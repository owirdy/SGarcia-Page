This paper introduces GraspGap, a framework for bridging the simulation-to-reality gap in dexterous robotic grasping. We propose a progressive training pipeline that combines domain randomization, adversarial domain adaptation, and real-world fine-tuning. Our method achieves state-of-the-art grasping performance on a multi-fingered robotic hand, significantly outperforming prior sim-to-real approaches on a diverse set of objects.
